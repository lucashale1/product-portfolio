## Problem
Even when candidates showed initial interest, activation dropped mid-journey.

The failure mode was not obvious: the UI felt “fine”, but candidates were hesitating at moments that required trust (e.g., confirming the tech approach early enough).

## Approach
I used a funnel-first mindset:
- Identify where drop-off spikes (activation funnel).
- Validate with behavioural session data (what candidates tried, struggled with, and abandoned).
- Convert hypotheses into controlled experiments with metrics that map to real outcomes.

## Execution
- Built a structured experiment plan using an impact/effort lens (so high-traffic experiments answered the highest-uncertainty questions first).
- Ran A/B tests on the onboarding experience, focusing on:
  - upfront tech confirmation
  - clarity of expectations before candidates commit time
  - removing UX friction and ambiguity
- Used PostHog event tracking to attribute results to specific stages and variants.

## Impact (metrics)
- Improved activation from **30% to 45%**
- Drove the change across **10,000+ candidates**

## Learnings
- When trust is required, “small wording/flow tweaks” can have outsized conversion impact.
- Funnel analysis only becomes useful when paired with behavioural evidence (session patterns) and clear stage attribution.

## What I’d do next
- Personalize onboarding by candidate baseline (so each candidate sees the right level of detail at the right time).
- Add experiment guardrails to detect adverse effects early (quality, downstream acceptance, and retention).
