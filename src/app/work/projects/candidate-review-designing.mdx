## Problem
Reviewing candidates is time-consuming and subjective. Teams need to:
- Compare candidates efficiently across stages.
- Reduce inconsistency between reviewers.
- Preserve transparency so decisions feel fair and explainable.

The product’s challenge wasn’t just “building UI”, it was designing a workflow that makes high-quality review the easiest path.

## Approach
I approached this as a “decision workflow” problem:
- Research how reviewers actually work under time pressure.
- Define a lightweight rubric that turns qualitative signals into consistent evaluation.
- Use AI assistance to surface structured summaries and candidate highlights, without hiding the underlying reasoning.

## Execution
- Created reviewer journey maps and ran targeted discovery conversations to understand friction points and handoff moments.
- Designed the review workflow in Figma (review queue, per-candidate summary, evidence-first sections, and decision actions).
- Validated key screens with rapid prototypes (Cursor/Claude Code-assisted iteration + quick feedback loops) before engineering handoff.
- Partnered with engineering to wire AI-assisted review surfaces and instrument PostHog event tracking to learn:
  - review start rate
  - review completion time
  - rubric usage vs skip behaviour

## Learnings
- Review workflows only stick when AI assistance is clearly controllable and transparent.
- Instrumentation needs to measure both “usage” and “decision quality”, otherwise teams optimize for the wrong behaviours.

## What I’d do next
- Finalize success metrics with engineering + stakeholders (time-to-review, rubric adherence, and downstream acceptance/quality).
- Add calibration loops (periodic reviewer alignment prompts) to keep rubric interpretation consistent.
- Improve AI evidence citation so reviewers can quickly verify key claims.
