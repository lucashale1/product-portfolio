## Problem
Candidates want to perform well, but interview prep is hard to translate into action. The journey often breaks down at the moment they need feedback and clear next steps.

From the hiring side, lower interview acceptance means more scheduling churn and slower hiring cycles.

## Approach
I treated this as a value-delivery problem (not just an AI feature): candidates needed feedback that was immediately actionable.

My product framing:
- Learn the candidate’s intent and interview context through lightweight discovery.
- Give coaching in the language of improvement (what to change and how).
- Measure the outcome that matters: interview acceptance, not “engagement” vanity metrics.

## Execution
- Wrote user stories and acceptance criteria tied to candidate outcomes.
- Designed the experience in Figma and used rapid prototypes to validate the flow before engineering handoff.
- Built/validated prototypes with Cursor/Claude Code to de-risk key interactions early.
- Partnered with engineering to define the integration points for the AI video coaching pipeline and event tracking.
- Implemented custom PostHog tracking from scratch to attribute outcomes to each stage of the coaching journey.

## Impact (metrics)
- Improved interview acceptance rates by **20%**
- Achieved **92% positive feedback** from candidates
- Part of the broader 0→1 product motion that reached **8,000+ monthly active users**

## Learnings
- Candidate outcomes improve when value is delivered quickly: “prep” needs to become “next action” within the first session.
- AI features require strong UX guardrails to earn trust (clear expectations, fast feedback, and concrete improvement steps).

## What I’d do next
- Expand coaching personalization (adapt feedback by role, interview format, and candidate baseline).
- Add an “explainability layer” so candidates understand why specific changes are recommended.
- Run follow-up experiments focused on the stage that drives the biggest conversion to acceptance.
